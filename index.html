Unconventions

It's ok to not follow common conventions.

I know, I know, someone made a website! They told the world that everyone should do Semantic Versioning, Conventional Commits, and also they should Keep a Changelog. The website looks nice and the idea itself even has a version!

Well, it's a convention. That's fine, and please follow it if you want to. Not everyone will follow it, and that's a good thing. That's how we get better conventions, for example. It's also fine to not be so serious, and sometimes do programming Just for Fun. No, really.

So if someone comes to you and asks why you don't follow <insert convention here> you can say that you choose to follow "Unconventions, version 無限" instead.
